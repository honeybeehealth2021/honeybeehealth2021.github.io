Maeva TECHER, PhD

Honey bee and Mites Population Genomics
Postdoc and JSPS Fellow, Jul. 2016 - March. 2021

Hey, thanks for stopping by and we hope you will enjoy this event! I started working with population genetics to retrace the evolution of insular honey bee populations with human arrival. While my first encounter with devastating Varroa mites was in Okinawa, I quickly became chatty about their success, to my surprise. So if you would like to learn more or exchange about Varroa world invasion genomics and evolution do not hesitate to email me.

Contact me at maeva.techer@oist.jp

Vienna KOWALLIK, PhD

Honey bee Microbiome and Environmental Stressors
Postdoc and DFG Fellow, Feb. 2017 - June. 2021

I have always been fascinated by microbial communities, specifically by the complexity of all the biotic and abiotic interactions and how this is impacting everything around us from organism's ecology and evolution to ecosystem functioning. Using a combination of controlled lab experiments and sequencing strategies, I study host-microbiome relationships in the honey bee system with a focus on how stress factors can impact microbiome composition and function for the host.

Contact me at vienna.kowallik@oist.jp

Nurit ELIASH, PhD

Honey bee - Mites Co-Evolution
Postdoc Ecology and Evolution, Oct. 2019 - Aug. 2021

Hi! Im interested in Bee-Varroa-Virus interactions, and their reciprocal co-evolution. Formerly investigating Varroa-Bee chemical communication I now focus on the Varroa-Virus, and Varroa-Bee interaction combining physiological, molecular studies and meta-omics analysis.

Contact me at nurit.eliah@oist.jp
